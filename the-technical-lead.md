# The Technical Lead

> What would you say you do here?
> - Bob Slydell, Office Space

I was given the title of "Technical Lead" in 2021 and I've been thinking about this question ever since. What is it that a technical lead does? I've finally come up with an answer that feels right to me.

There are three fundamental forces that every technical lead needs to monitor and manage:
 * The Mission: How does your department help the buisiness as a whole? What are the stated goals? What services / pipelines / databases / et cetera need to be maintained and improved?
 * The Team: The people that build the systems. Each engineer has their own knowledge, specializations, and motivations. Who are they?
 * The Executives: Whoever decides if your team as a whole is doing well (promotions, headcount, projects) or poorly (re-orgs and layoffs). The people that make the big decisions.

The job of a technical lead is to make sure that these three forces are pushing in the same direction. We don't have outright control over any of these forces, but we can (must!) influence each of them to be successful.

Let's consider a choice between improving loading times or working on a feature for the widgets team. All three forces will be a factor when deciding which project happens first. Simple choices like this are the bread and butter of day to day technical leadership.

 * The Mission: What does the roadmap say about each project? Which one will improve metrics? Which project fits into the stated goals for this sprint?
 * The Team: Who is available, and which would they prefer to work on? Does your team like helping out, or will the request be a bother? Can Alice get this done or do we need Bob too?
 * The Executives:  Will anyone important get mad if we delay the widget project? How well can you sell each project to your boss's boss? Which makes your team look better?

What should I do next here? I can talk to my team and see who is available. I can have a chat with the widgets manager and make sure I understand their feature better, or a chat with other leads to check if they have more context. I can look at metrics or user studies to understand the buisiness need, or I can think back to the last all hands meeting and remeber what (if anything) the executives have been saying about the widgets project. If both projects are really urgent I can make a case to my director for why we need to hire someone or pull in extra hands from other areas.

Each force changes over time. The mission changes with each new project and planning cycle. The team changes as  engineers develop skills, get burned out, and change roles. Executive focus and priorities shift over time. How will the choice in front of me shape each fundamental force? Which choice (and which _method_ for making that choice) keeps all three forces well aligned?

That is my job. I keep close tabs on buisiness needs, I know what my team is doing, and I know where the executives are focused. I know how to navigate the hundreds of simple decisions like this one that go into my companies products. When to make a call, when to escalate, how to say no diplomatically, and how to line up this project for my team so that I can move on to the next choice in line.
